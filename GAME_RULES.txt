# Card Game Rules

## Overview
This is a strategic two-player card game where the objective is to achieve the **lowest total points** by the end of the game. Players must manage their hidden cards, use special card powers strategically, and decide when to end the game.

---

## Game Setup

### Deck Composition
- **Standard Cards**: 52 playing cards (A-K in all four suits: ‚ô†, ‚ô•, ‚ô¶, ‚ô£)
- **Jokers**: 2 Jokers
- **Total**: 54 cards

### Initial Deal
1. The deck is shuffled with randomized key assignments (1-54)
2. Each player receives **2 cards** face down
3. Remaining cards form the draw pile
4. Players may look at **one** of their two cards at the start of the game

## Gameplay

### Turn Structure
The game proceeds in alternating turns between Player 1 and Player 2.

#### Each Turn Consists Of:
1. **Draw Phase**: Draw the top card from the deck
2. **Decision Phase**: Choose to keep or discard the drawn card
   - **Keep**: Replace one of your two cards with the drawn card (discarded card goes to graveyard)
   - **Discard**: Discard the drawn card to graveyard and keep both original cards
3. **Special Power Phase**: If you discarded a special card (10, J, Q, K), you may use its power
4. **Show Phase**: Decide whether to call "Show" to end the game

## Ending the Game

### "Show" Declaration
At the end of each turn, the active player can declare **"Show"** to end the game.

#### Show Rules:
- **If Player 1 declares Show first**:
  - Player 2 does NOT get a final turn
  - Game ends immediately

- **If Player 2 declares Show first**:
  - Player 1 gets ONE final turn
  - Game then ends

### Automatic End
- If the draw pile is depleted (no cards left), the game ends in a **tie**

---

## Winning Conditions

### Point Calculation
At the end of the game:
1. All cards are revealed
2. Each player's two cards are totaled using the points system
3. The player with the **LOWER total** wins

### Winner Determination
- **Player with fewer points**: WINS üèÜ
- **Equal points**: TIE ü§ù
- **Deck depleted**: TIE ü§ù

## Strategy Tips

### General Strategy
- **Low cards are valuable**: Aces (1 point) and low numbers are your best cards
- **Red Kings are golden**: K‚ô• and K‚ô¶ worth 0 points - extremely valuable!
- **Jokers help**: -1 point means they reduce your total
- **High cards are dangerous**: Face cards (especially black Kings at 13 points) hurt your score

### Information Management
- Remember which card you looked at initially
- Use 10s and Jacks to gain information before making swaps
- Track what cards have been discarded to infer what's left

### Timing Your Show
- Call "Show" when you believe you have lower points than your opponent
- Be cautious - if Player 2, remember Player 1 gets a final turn
- Don't wait too long - opponent might improve their hand

### Special Power Usage
- **10s**: Use early to know your hand better
- **Jacks**: Use to scout opponent before attempting swaps
- **Queens**: High risk/reward - only use if desperate or confident
- **Kings**: Best power - use wisely for optimal swaps

## Technical Notes

### State Representation (for AI/RL Training)
The game state is represented as a 5-element tensor:
- `[card_1, card_2, opp_card_1, opp_card_2, decision_code]`
- Unknown cards: -1
- Last element: Decision type code (60-71)

### Decision Codes
- 60: Player 1 initial card peek choice
- 61: Player 2 initial card peek choice
- 62: Player 1 show decision
- 63: Player 2 show decision
- 64: Keep or discard drawn card
- 65: Which card to swap
- 66: Use special power
- 67: 10's power - which own card to see
- 68: Jack's power - which opponent card to see
- 69-71: Queen/King related (currently disabled)
